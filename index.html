SPECIAL KERALA FESTIVAL TOURS

Kathakali

Kathakali is a classical dance-drama form that originated in Kerala, known for its vibrant costumes, elaborate makeup, and expressive gestures. It tells stories from Indian epics like the Mahabharata and Ramayana, blending dance, music, and acting into a mesmerizing performance. The dancers, adorned with intricate face paint and headgear, convey emotions through precise facial expressions and hand gestures, making Kathakali a captivating cultural experience.

Thrissur Pooram

Thrissur Pooram is Kerala's grandest temple festival, celebrated annually in the city of Thrissur. Known as the "mother of all Poorams," it features a spectacular display of decorated elephants, traditional percussion music (Panchavadyam), and magnificent fireworks. The highlight is the Kudamattam, a colorful umbrella-swapping ceremony between competing temples. Thrissur Pooram is a vibrant cultural extravaganza that attracts thousands of devotees and tourists every year.

Kerala Boat Race

Kerala Boat Race Kerala's boat races, especially the famous Nehru Trophy Boat Race in Alappuzha, are thrilling water sports events held during the monsoon season. Teams of rowers compete in long, snake-shaped boats called "chundan vallams," moving in perfect synchrony to the rhythm of traditional songs. The boat races, deeply rooted in Kerala's culture, are a celebration of community spirit and tradition, drawing large crowds to witness the exhilarating spectacle.
